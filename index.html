What I Do

Sustainability analysis for complex integrated systems.

My portfolio is built around one theme: using data, models, and decision support analysis to make sustainability decisions more transparent, defensible, and implementation-ready.

01

Sustainability and environmental analysis

I evaluate environmental impacts, emissions drivers, resource constraints, lifecycle hotspots, and deployment risks for low-carbon systems, with emphasis on practical decisions rather than isolated metrics.

02

GIS, data analytics, and spatial intelligence

I integrate land use, soil, infrastructure, demographic, socio-economic, environmental justice, and logistics data to identify feasible locations, spatial hotspots, exposure risks, and opportunity zones.

03

Decision support analysis and consulting-style modeling

I build decision-support workflows that connect technical performance, economics, environmental impacts, social LCA, community benefits, and implementation risk into clear outputs for planning and strategy.

04

Social analysis and implementation risk

I assess how sustainability decisions affect communities, workers, regional economies, and underserved populations using social LCA, socio-economic indicators, I-O modeling, and environmental justice screening.

How I Work

From messy data to defensible recommendations.

I structure projects like decision problems: define the question, build the evidence base, model tradeoffs, evaluate uncertainty, and translate results into actionable recommendations.

01

Frame the decision

Clarify what choice the analysis supports, such as siting, screening, risk reduction, or investment prioritization.

02

Build the data layer

Curate spatial, environmental, operational, cost, emissions, and socio-economic datasets into an analysis-ready structure.

03

Model tradeoffs

Use GIS, Python, TEA, LCA, social LCA, logistics modeling, MCDA, hotspot analysis, sensitivity analysis, and scenario planning to quantify cost, impact, feasibility, equity, risk, and uncertainty.

04

Communicate outputs

Translate technical results into maps, dashboards, ranked alternatives, risk matrices, sensitivity plots, implementation roadmaps, and decision-ready summaries.

Decision support analysis

Integrated model for sustainable infrastructure siting

Problem

Where should energy crop supply and SAF-related infrastructure be deployed when land, logistics, economics, environmental impact, and community context all matter at the same time?

Approach

Designed a Python-based decision support model that connects crop production, transportation and logistics, biorefinery process modeling, TEA, LCA, social LCA, socio-economic analysis, GIS, and visualization layers.

Output

Decision-ready outputs that compare feasible regions, quantify tradeoffs, identify environmental and social hotspots, and support site screening for investors, researchers, public-sector stakeholders, and community-centered planning.

Impact

Moves sustainability analysis beyond single-metric feasibility by linking technical, economic, environmental, spatial, social, and implementation-risk dimensions into one systematic workflow.

Spatial intelligence and hotspot analysis

Layered GIS screening for regional hotspots and opportunity zones

Problem

County-level averages hide local variation. For real deployment decisions, analysts need a way to combine land, environmental, infrastructure, and socio-economic criteria at finer spatial resolution.

Approach

Built a layer-by-layer spatial screening workflow that adds decision criteria, applies multi-criteria decision analysis, computes suitability, and identifies candidate regions that satisfy land, infrastructure, environmental, and socio-economic constraints.

Output

Ranked and mapped hotspots, opportunity zones, and constrained regions that can guide deeper feasibility analysis, stakeholder engagement, risk screening, and scenario comparison.

Impact

Provides a transparent analytical structure for consulting-style recommendations, not just maps. The same logic can support renewable energy, waste, land use, and environmental planning problems.

Sustainability, TEA, LCA, and social LCA

Integrated TEA, LCA, social LCA, and scenario analysis

Problem

Sustainability projects often fail when feasibility is judged too narrowly. Cost, emissions, supply risk, land constraints, environmental justice, worker and community impacts, and implementation risk need to be assessed together.

Approach

Developed system-level models to estimate lifecycle emissions, environmental impacts, economic feasibility, social performance indicators, and uncertainty under changing assumptions such as land rent, discount rate, yield, logistics, and operating conditions.

Output

Scenario results, sensitivity analysis, normalized cost and emissions metrics, social risk indicators, contribution analysis, and interpretation of key drivers affecting project feasibility.

Impact

Supports sustainability strategy by showing which assumptions matter most, where lifecycle hotspots occur, and where operational, policy, or community-focused changes can improve outcomes.
